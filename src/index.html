ŠIBENIK

The oldest native Croatian town on the shores of the sea.
Šibenik, a gem in the Croatian Adriatic, is situated along the mouth of Krka on the most picturesque part of the eastern Adriatic coast.

It was first mentioned in 1066 during the reign of the Croatian King Kresimir IV.

NATURE

Roughly 18 kilometres (11 mi) north of the city is the Krka National Park, renowned for its many waterfalls, flora, fauna, and historical and archaeological remains.
The Kornati archipelago, west of Šibenik, consists of 150 islands in a sea area of about 320 km2 (124 sq mi), making it the densest archipelago in the Mediterranean Sea.

MAIN SIGHTS

One of the things not to be missed in Sibenik is Sibenik’s cathedral – Cathedral Sveti Jakov, built back to 1434, is an important architectural Renaissance building in Croatia that is listed by Unesco World Heritage.
This cathedral was built by of lime stone and marble from the stone mason’s Island of Brac in Croatia.
